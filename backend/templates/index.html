How to Find Air Pollution Data

To get accurate pollution data for your area, here are several reliable sources and methods:

Government Monitoring Stations

  • CPCB India: Central Pollution Control Board
  • AQI: AQI
  • SAFAR: System of Air Quality Forecasting
  • State PCBs: Check your state pollution control board website

Look for stations near your location for local data

Use AQI - Recommended

Mobile Applications

  • AirVisual: Real-time air quality and forecasts
  • Plume Labs: Hyperlocal pollution tracking
  • BreezoMeter: Detailed pollution breakdown
  • SAFAR-Air: India-specific air quality app
  • CPCB's Sameer: Official Indian AQI app

Apps provide hourly updates and forecasts

International Platforms

  • World Air Quality Index: waqi.info
  • IQAir: Global air quality
  • OpenAQ: Open air quality data
  • NASA Worldview: Satellite data

Global platforms often have Indian city data

Understanding Pollutant Levels

Important: Divide ppb by 1000 to get ppm (for CO conversion).

Pollutant Good Level Moderate Unhealthy Units Sources
PM2.5 (Fine Particles) 0-12 μg/m³ 12-35 μg/m³ 35+ μg/m³ μg/m³ Vehicles, Industry, Construction
PM10 (Coarse Particles) 0-54 μg/m³ 54-154 μg/m³ 154+ μg/m³ μg/m³ Dust, Pollen, Construction
NO₂ (Nitrogen Dioxide) 0-40 ppb 40-80 ppb 80+ ppb ppb Vehicle exhaust, Power plants
SO₂ (Sulfur Dioxide) 0-20 ppb 20-75 ppb 75+ ppb ppb Industrial processes, Coal burning
CO (Carbon Monoxide) 0.1-0.4 ppm 0.4-0.9 ppm 0.9+ ppm ppm Vehicle exhaust, Incomplete combustion
O₃ (Ozone) 0-54 ppb 54-70 ppb 70+ ppb ppb Chemical reactions in sunlight

Understanding Air Pollution Health Impacts

Air pollution poses serious threats to human health, causing various diseases through long-term exposure to pollutants like PM2.5, PM10, NO₂, SO₂, CO, and O₃.

Common Pollution-Related Diseases

Asthma

Chronic airway inflammation causing wheezing, coughing, and breathing difficulties. Triggered by pollutants like PM2.5 and O₃.

Bronchitis

Inflammation of bronchial tubes leading to persistent cough, mucus production, and chest discomfort.

COPD

Chronic obstructive pulmonary disease causing progressive breathing difficulties and reduced lung function.

Lung Cancer

Malignant lung tumor development often linked to long-term exposure to carcinogenic air pollutants.

Pneumonia

Lung infection exacerbated by air pollution, causing inflammation and fluid accumulation in air sacs.

Heart Disease

Increased cardiovascular risks including heart attacks, arrhythmias, and heart failure due to pollution.

Stroke

Cerebrovascular events triggered by particulate matter affecting blood flow to the brain.

Allergic Rhinitis

Nasal inflammation causing sneezing, congestion, and runny nose due to airborne allergens.

Eye Irritation

Redness, itching, and watering eyes caused by direct exposure to pollutants and smog.

Chronic Cough

Persistent cough lasting weeks or months, often resulting from prolonged pollution exposure.

Reduced Lung Function

Gradual decline in lung capacity and breathing efficiency due to long-term pollution exposure.
